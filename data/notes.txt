What is stress?
Stress is the body’s response to pressure or challenges. It can be helpful in small amounts but becomes harmful when it feels constant or overwhelming.

Coping strategies for stress:
- Practice deep breathing for a few minutes.
- Break large tasks into smaller and easier steps.
- Go for a short walk outside or do light stretching.
- Talk to someone you trust about what’s worrying you.

What is anxiety?
Anxiety is a feeling of worry or fear about future events or uncertainty. It can cause restlessness and rapid thoughts.

Coping strategies for anxiety:
- Try grounding techniques: name 5 things you can see, 4 things you can feel, 3 you can hear.
- Slow breathing: inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4, exhale for 4.
- Reduce screen time, especially before sleep.
- Write down your worries and challenge negative thoughts gently.

What is social anxiety?
Social anxiety is fear or nervousness in social situations like talking to others, meeting new people, or being observed.

Coping strategies for social anxiety:
- Practice small exposure steps like greeting one person.
- Breathe slowly when you feel nervous.
- Focus on listening instead of worrying about your performance.

What is sadness?
Sadness is a normal emotion that happens when something hurts you emotionally. It may come and go.

Coping strategies for sadness:
- Allow yourself to express your feelings.
- Do something comforting like listening to music or having a warm drink.
- Write about what you feel in a journal.
- Spend a few minutes outside in fresh air.

What is loneliness?
Loneliness is the feeling of being disconnected or lacking support, even if others are around you.

Coping strategies for loneliness:
- Contact a friend or family member for even a short chat.
- Engage in creative activities you enjoy.
- Join an online group or community activity.

What is depression?
Depression is a prolonged period of sadness and loss of interest in activities. It affects mood and motivation.

Wellness support for low mood:
- Create a tiny routine: morning sunlight, drinking water, short walk.
- Practice gratitude: note 3 things you appreciate each day.
- Talk to someone supportive.
(If someone is in crisis or speaks about self-harm, encourage seeking professional help.)

What is overthinking?
Overthinking means repeatedly worrying about the same issue. It can increase stress and mental exhaustion.

Coping strategies for overthinking:
- Distract your mind with a helpful task like cleaning your room or listening to music.
- Practice mindfulness: focus on what you see, hear, smell right now.
- Set a “worry time” for just 10 minutes, then stop.

What is nervousness?
Nervousness is a temporary reaction to stress or upcoming events like exams or interviews.

Coping strategies for nervousness:
- Deep breathing to calm your mind.
- Prepare ahead and focus on one thing at a time.
- Practice positive self-talk: “I will do my best.”

What is anger?
Anger is a strong emotion when things feel unfair or frustrating.

Coping strategies for anger:
- Take a walk or move your body to release tension.
- Write down what made you angry and pause before reacting.
- Practice counting slowly from 1 to 10.

What is boredom?
Boredom happens when we lack interest or stimulation.

Coping strategies for boredom:
- Try learning something new or picking up a hobby.
- Read, draw, listen to music, or go outside.
- Do a quick workout or stretch.

What is tiredness or fatigue?
Feeling tired can come from lack of sleep, stress, or overworking.

Coping strategies for tiredness:
- Maintain a regular sleep routine.
- Drink enough water.
- Take short breaks when doing long tasks.

What is fear?
Fear appears when we feel threatened or uncertain.

Coping strategies for fear:
- Talk to someone about your worries.
- Challenge fearful thoughts gently with facts.
- Practice relaxation exercises.

What is burnout?
Burnout is extreme exhaustion caused by prolonged stress, especially related to school or work.

Coping strategies for burnout:
- Take frequent short breaks during work.
- Ask for help when overloaded.
- Spend time on activities you enjoy.

What is panic?
Panic is a sudden rush of intense fear causing symptoms like rapid heartbeat or difficulty breathing.

Coping strategies for panic:
- Breathe slowly and focus on exhaling longer.
- Remind yourself: “This will pass and I am safe.”
- Sit in a calm environment.

What is confusion?
Feeling confused means difficulty thinking clearly, often due to stress or too many thoughts.

Coping strategies for confusion:
- Pause and take a few deep breaths.
- Write down your tasks in order of priority.
- Take a short break to reset your mind.

What is lack of motivation?
It means not feeling the urge to do tasks even if they are important.

Coping strategies for low motivation:
- Start with just one small step.
- Reward yourself after completing tasks.
- Do simple activities you enjoy briefly.

What is positivity?
Positivity is the mindset of focusing on good aspects of life, even during challenges.

Ways to maintain positivity:
- Maintain healthy habits and stay connected with people.
- Celebrate small achievements every day.
- Practice gratitude journaling.

Helpful mindfulness exercises:
- Focus on breathing for 5 minutes.
- Observe your surroundings without judgment.
- Try short guided meditation audios.

Relaxation techniques:
- Progressive muscle relaxation (tighten then relax muscles).
- Listening to soothing music.
- Stretching your body slowly.

When to seek professional help?
- If feelings become too heavy or last a long time.
- If you feel hopeless, helpless, or unsafe.
- If daily functioning becomes very difficult.
A trained counselor or helpline can support you confidentially.
